Welcome to the AIonOS NTPC Procurement Agent walkthrough. In this version, the video pacing is slower so each screen can be understood before moving forward.

On the opening dashboard, you are seeing one unified operating view built from existing SAP procurement records. Contracts, purchase orders, dispatch status, inventory cover, plant consumption, logistics movement, and quality indicators are all visible together. This removes report switching and gives planners one current baseline.

On the next screen, the supplier insight panel explains performance by source. For each coal company, the agent compares grade, GCV band, moisture and ash behavior, committed quantity versus delivered quantity, transit lead time, and route consistency. Landed cost is shown as a full stack, separating base coal cost from freight and handling, so commercial teams can spot the true cost drivers.

As we move to the risk view, the system highlights where action is needed: contract slippage, dispatch delay, route congestion, quality drift, and low-cover plants. Instead of only flagging red alerts, the agent explains why each issue matters for generation continuity.

Now we enter scenario simulation. This screen lets teams model realistic disruptions, including wagon shortage, weather delay, source curtailment, sudden demand rise, or grade downgrade. For every simulated case, the platform recalculates sourcing mix, route allocation, projected landed cost, and days-of-stock by plant.

On the optimization screen, the agent recommends the best procurement plan across approved suppliers. The recommendation balances cost, quality compliance, reliability, and transport feasibility. Every recommendation is explainable, so teams can approve directly or adjust assumptions with full transparency.

Finally, on the forecasting and execution screen, the system converts planned coal receipts into expected generation potential. If a gap is predicted, the workflow triggers targeted notifications: suppliers receive expedite or reallocation requests, and plants receive blending and burn-plan guidance. This closes the loop from insight to simulation to optimized execution.

That is the NTPC Procurement Agent on AIonOS: a clearer, decision-ready command layer that helps teams make faster and safer coal-to-power procurement decisions.
