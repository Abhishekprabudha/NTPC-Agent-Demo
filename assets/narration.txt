Welcome to the AIonOS NTPC Procurement Agent. In this walkthrough, we follow how procurement teams move from fragmented SAP records to an AI-guided coal planning command center.

The journey starts with dashboard unification. The agent connects existing SAP procurement, contracts, inventory, plant consumption, logistics, supplier master, and quality datasets into one operational cockpit. Instead of switching between reports, teams see live demand, stock cover, pending POs, dispatch status, rake movement, and landed-cost drivers on a single screen.

Next, the insight layer explains what the data means. The system compares coal companies by grade, GCV range, moisture and ash profile, committed quantity, delivered quantity, transit reliability, lead time, and total landed cost including freight and handling. It surfaces risk hotspots such as underperforming suppliers, contract slippage, quality deviation, route congestion, and inventory vulnerability at plant level.

Then we run simulation scenarios. Users can model disruption cases like delayed dispatch, wagon shortages, weather-linked logistics delays, quality downgrade, demand surge from peak load, or sudden supply curtailment at a source mine. For each scenario, the agent recomputes sourcing mix, route allocation, expected cost impact, and days-of-stock resilience for every plant.

From these simulations, the optimization engine recommends the best procurement plan. It balances cost, availability, quality compliance, reliability score, and transport feasibility across Coal India subsidiaries and other approved suppliers. The recommendation is transparent, with explainable trade-offs so teams can approve, adjust, or override with confidence.

Finally, the forecasting layer estimates power generation potential from available and planned coal receipts. As forecast demand and generation gaps are detected, the system automatically triggers supplier notifications for expediting or reallocation and plant notifications for blending strategy, conservation actions, or revised burn plans. The result is a closed-loop workflow: insight to simulation, simulation to optimization, and optimization to proactive execution.

That is the NTPC Procurement Agent on AIonOS—an intelligent procurement copilot that turns SAP data into faster, safer, and more cost-effective coal-to-power decisions.
